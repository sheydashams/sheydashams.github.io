Why I'm Running.

Sheyda Shams. As EngSoc President? Yes.

I chose to run for President because I want to represent the society and its students. It is important to me to make sure the students feel they can trust and bring forward their concerns and recommendations to their President and for the President to always put the best interest of the students and faculty first. It is in my nature to be a leader and as a student of this Faculty, it would be a great honour to get to represent everyone as best as I can. Having been one of the academic representatives for my program since Spring 2021 along with my involvement with the Engineering Society as secretary, speaker, EDI director, and being the VP Communications for the Conference on Diversity in Engineering at UWaterloo will be hosting in 2023, I believe I can bring a bright and new outlook to this position.

Goals.

Although I have many goals and my goals will evolve and adjust to situational elements, my three big areas of focus are EDI Education, Promotion of Mental Wellness, and Student Spaces.

Equity, Diversity & Inclusivity

It is immensely important that Waterloo Engineering Society (EngSoc) along with the Faculty of Engineering be a welcoming entity in terms of EDI. For EngSoc/Faculty to be as inclusive as possible, it is pertinent that it be as knowledgeable, well-versed, and educated.

My goal is to increase EDI awareness events/workshops and to hopefully see an increase of representation in council team (class reps, directors, commissioners/officers, and executive team).

Mental Wellness & Stress

We all get overwhelmed sometimes and struggle with managing our stress levels, decreasing productivity and negatively impact mental health. As students with busy schedules and busy lives, it is important to actively promote mental wellness and different ways we can spend time and focus on ourselves.

My goal is to have students feel comfortable incorporating ways to help them ease stress in their school lives along with learning and being able to explore different ways to do so.


Student Spaces

As we transition back to in-person and welcome the 2027s in the Fall, student spaces should be lively and welcoming spaces where students can socialize and relax. Coming out of the pandemic, it is important to promote the social aspect of our education and foster student connections.

My goal is to have either events or public promotion of these spaces for new and returning students to regain the in-person feeling of community.
